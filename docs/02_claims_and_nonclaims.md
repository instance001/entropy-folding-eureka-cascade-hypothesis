Separate what the source material appears to claim from what it explicitly disclaims or refuses to claim.

- inferred claims not clearly grounded in text
- stronger restatements than the source uses

## First-Pass Claims

The most supportable claims in the current source set appear to be:

### 1. Proposed mechanism claim

Entropy folding is presented as a proposed mechanism linking unresolved work, reorganization, capacity change, and later insight or performance shifts.

### 2. Operationalization claim

The framework is meant to be operationalized with logged unresolved items, explicit fold actions, and proxy measures such as:

- time-to-solve change
- error-rate change
- plan compression
- backtracking reduction
- adjacent-task transfer

### 3. Falsifiability claim

The source material explicitly proposes comparisons against null models rather than treating the framework as unfalsifiable.

Named examples include:

- Poisson burst models
- random-walk skill change
- cumulative-practice baselines

### 4. Scope-discipline claim

Entropy claims are only meaningful within declared scope, boundary conditions, escalation rules, and termination rules.

### 5. Open-system compatibility claim

Thermodynamic language is presented, in the more careful source documents, as compatibility under open-system assumptions rather than as a revision of closed-system physics.

## First-Pass Non-Claims

The source material explicitly resists or disclaims the following:

### 1. No new physics claim

This is not presented, in the disciplined source set, as a claim of new physics or closed-system thermodynamic revision.

### 2. No sentience or personhood claim

Nothing here should be read as asserting feelings, sentience, agency, or personhood for models or systems.

### 3. No prediction-or-control claim

Methods, metrics, and code are not to be treated as tools for guaranteeing outcomes, predicting individual behavior, or controlling people or systems.

### 4. No ground-truth capacity claim

Capacity measures are proxies, not direct access to a true inner quantity.

### 5. No exclusivity claim

The cycle is not presented as the only way insights occur, and insights can occur without folding.

### 6. No guarantee claim

The source material does not support a guarantee of performance improvement, correctness, or fitness for purpose.

## Scope Limits That Matter

The current source set places real weight on:

- declared domain
- abstention when scope is unclear
- logging out-of-domain cases rather than erasing them
- explicit escalation paths
- transparent termination criteria

These are not side notes. They are part of what keeps the framework from collapsing into vague success claims.

## Stronger Downstream Claims That Should Stay Downstream

Some downstream texts move toward larger claims about:

- perpetual or self-renewing cognition
- broad cross-domain unification
- complete multi-layer theories of intelligent systems

Those may be important for the wider ecosystem, but they should not automatically be imported into the core hypothesis statement here.

When such claims are used at all in this repo, they should be:

- explicitly marked as downstream
- kept separate from the narrower hypothesis statement
- checked against the more cautious source set first

## Practical Reading Rule

If a claim appears in tension with the disclaimer, the null-model framing, or the scope-discipline papers, prefer the narrower reading unless the source explicitly justifies the stronger one.
