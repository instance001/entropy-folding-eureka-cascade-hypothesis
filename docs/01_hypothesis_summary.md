## First-Pass Summary

In the most disciplined parts of the source corpus, entropy folding is presented as a proposed mechanism for understanding how unresolved complexity can be stored, reorganized, and later resolved more effectively rather than as a discardable failure state.

The cleanest working version of the hypothesis is:

- unresolved items are logged or retained rather than erased
- those unresolved items can be reorganized through explicit folding operations
- that reorganization may increase effective structural capacity, tracked only through proxies
- later resolutions may then arrive with lower effort or as discrete performance jumps

In that framing, the core cycle is roughly:

1. unresolved items are vaulted
2. stall is recognized rather than hidden
3. the vaulted material is reorganized
4. capacity change is estimated with proxies
5. later insight or resolution is observed
6. resulting outputs feed the next cycle

The source material repeatedly treats this as:

- a research program
- a framework for operationalization
- a falsifiable set of claims about burst patterns, transfer, or capacity proxies

It does not need to be read as:

- a claim of new physics
- a claim that all insight works this way
- a guarantee of performance gain
- a theory of prediction or control over individuals

## Minimal Working Interpretation

For a serious outside reader, the safest first interpretation is:

Entropy folding is a structured hypothesis that stalled or unresolved work may sometimes function as deferred structure rather than pure waste, and that disciplined reorganization of that deferred material may be associated with later gains in problem-solving performance.

That interpretation is narrower than some downstream language in the ecosystem, but it is better supported by the most explicit cautionary and operational documents.

## Relationship To Eureka Cascade Language

At this stage, the safest reading is that "eureka cascade" language belongs to the downstream description of what repeated or clustered insight events may look like if the entropy-folding cycle is real and if similar fold dynamics recur across tasks or scales.

This repo therefore treats:

- entropy folding as the more basic hypothesis term
- eureka cascade as a downstream descriptive label unless a stronger equivalence is made explicit in the source material

If later source reading shows a tighter formal relation, that should be documented explicitly rather than assumed.

## Working Mechanism For This Consolidation Pass

For this consolidation pass, the working mechanism is:

1. a system encounters a constraint, blockage, contradiction, overload, or unresolved problem it cannot metabolize immediately
2. the unresolved material becomes vaulted rather than solved
3. the system pivots, routes around the blockage, or moves elsewhere
4. later, the system re-encounters the vaulted material from a different angle, scale, context, topology, or catalyst
5. if the catalyst or topology fits, the previously stuck material metabolizes quickly
6. one unlock may expose adjacent vaults, producing compounding integration or a eureka cascade

In compact form:

`stuck -> vaulted -> pivot -> catalyst fit -> unlock -> adjacent unlocks / cascade`

## What "Vaulted" Means In This Working Mechanism

In this repo, "vaulted" should be read broadly but still concretely.

It may include:

- deferral
- compression
- routing-around
- latent storage
- held contradiction
- unresolved structured backlog

It should not be used so broadly that every unfinished task automatically counts as a vault.

## What Counts As A Catalyst In This Working Mechanism

For this pass, a catalyst may be:

- a person
- a tool
- an idea
- a domain
- a prompt
- an environment
- an analogy
- a model
- another structural input

The key requirement is not novelty by itself.
The key requirement is fit: the catalyst must mesh with the existing unresolved structure strongly enough to make previously stuck material metabolizable.

## Why This Mechanism Matters

This mechanism is the measuring stick for the separate signal atlas.

A source should not rank highly in the atlas merely because it is:

- cross-domain
- analogy-shaped
- Symbound-adjacent
- thematically resonant

To count as a stronger atlas signal candidate, a source should show several of the following clearly:

1. stuckness, overload, contradiction, or unresolved tension
2. vaulting, deferral, routing-around, compression, or latent storage
3. later pivot, return, or changed angle/context/scale
4. catalyst or topology fit
5. rapid unlock, compounding insight, or cascading integration
6. evidence that this structure is central rather than decorative

## Upstream And Downstream Layers

The source corpus appears to separate several layers, even when not always cleanly:

- foundational layer: scope, scale, vectors, and other conceptual constraints
- operational layer: vaulting, folding, capacity change, insight, logging
- downstream reflection layer: broader integrations, analogies, or system-scale descriptions

This repository keeps the first two layers closest to the center of the hypothesis summary.
