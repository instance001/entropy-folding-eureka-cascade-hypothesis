## First-Pass Weakening And Falsifiability Frame

The current source corpus is at its strongest when it treats the hypothesis as vulnerable to failure, null-model comparison, and scope collapse.

## Named Falsifiability Routes In The Core Source

The most explicit falsifiability language currently comes from the directed-thermodynamics paper.

### 1. Burst shift beyond Poisson

If folding matters, burst timing or magnitude should deviate from Poisson expectations.

This is weakened or falsified if:

- burst statistics match Poisson expectations under folding and non-folding conditions
- apparent bursts are no more structured than constant-rate event baselines

### 2. Proxy improvement versus random walk

If the framework is meaningful, relevant capacity proxies should improve more after fold cycles than in matched random-walk simulations.

This is weakened or falsified if:

- post-fold improvements fall inside random-walk confidence bounds
- proxy changes cannot be distinguished from ordinary drift

### 3. Transfer bump beyond practice-only baselines

If folding contributes something beyond repetition, adjacent-task transfer should exceed simple cumulative-practice baselines.

This is weakened or falsified if:

- transfer gains are indistinguishable from practice alone
- matched tasks show no fold-specific advantage

## General Weakening Conditions

Even short of outright falsification, the hypothesis is weakened when:

- proxies are poorly chosen or unstable
- tasks are too weak to discriminate fold effects from ordinary practice
- null models are absent, weak, or misreported
- scope is vague enough that failure cases are silently excluded
- folding actions are not operationally logged
- claimed insights are retrospective storytelling rather than measurable shifts

## Scope-Based Failure Conditions

The foundational-scope material implies that claims weaken sharply when:

- domain `D` is not declared
- out-of-domain cases are hidden or erased
- abstention is replaced by forced folding
- escalation rules are absent
- termination conditions are unclear

In other words, a scope-undisciplined entropy-folding claim is weaker even before any experimental result is examined.

## Boundary And Failure Conditions From Downstream Limitation Material

The PCR limitation and boundary-condition documents are downstream, but still useful as caution-heavy material.

They suggest weakening conditions such as:

- recursion exceeding substrate capacity
- insufficient or trivial entropy
- excessive noise mistaken for meaningful entropy
- overwhelming entropy producing overload rather than structure
- misassigned salience
- gating failure
- unstable attractors
- overflow-routing failure

These do not all belong to the minimal upstream hypothesis itself, but they are useful as explicit candidate failure modes for later operational systems built on top of it.

## Formal Conditions That Suggest Weakening Or Rejection

Several source files include toy equations, threshold conditions, or formal sketches that imply concrete weakening conditions if not met.

Examples already present in source material include:

- scope conditions requiring `p(in D | x)` and risk thresholds before a fold is attempted
- scale conditions requiring local relief not to exceed budgeted macro leakage
- action conditions requiring expected task relief, bounded environmental leak, and cost-budget compliance
- energy conditions requiring entropy relief within a declared ledger
- ontic conditions requiring bounded measurement error and documented state mappings
- stability conditions requiring minimum deltas, cooldown windows, and bounded drift

These matter because they show that some parts of the corpus already imply:

- folds can be invalid
- actions can be rejected or re-scoped
- apparent relief can be treated as illusory if cross-scale or state-definition conditions fail

Current caution:

- these are existing source-side sketches and constraints
- they should not be unified into a single polished formal test unless the source itself does that explicitly

## Strong Practical Cautions

The hypothesis should be treated as weakened if any of the following recur:

- "insight" language appears without proxy tracking
- thermodynamic language is used without the open-system caveat
- capacity is discussed as if directly observed
- downstream architecture rhetoric substitutes for actual measurement
- outputs or projects are treated as confirmation simply because they exist

## Narrowest Responsible Reading

The narrowest responsible reading is:

- entropy folding remains a proposed mechanism
- it earns confidence only to the degree that logged fold cycles outperform stated null models under declared scope conditions
- it loses credibility when scope boundaries, measurement discipline, or failure reporting break down
